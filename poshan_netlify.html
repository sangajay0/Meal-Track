Regulatory Framework
Legal Basis
Poshan Path is built upon established government nutrition policy and peer-reviewed science.

PM POSHAN Shakti Nirman Scheme

The PM POSHAN scheme, notified under the National Food Security Act 2013, mandates nutritious hot cooked meals for all children enrolled in government and government-aided schools from Classes 1 to 8 across India. This application uses the scheme's minimum caloric guidelines as a baseline while extending targets to meet full ICMR 2020 Recommended Dietary Allowances.

Minimum Nutritional Standards

  • Primary (Classes 1–5): minimum 450 kcal and 12g protein per meal
  • Upper Primary (Classes 6–8): minimum 700 kcal and 20g protein per meal
  • Poshan Path targets values above this baseline for optimal child development

Iron Deficiency Anaemia

NFHS-5 (2019–21) reports anaemia in 67% of children aged 6–59 months. Iron is tracked as a priority micronutrient throughout this application, with particular focus on adequate intake for adolescent girls who are at highest risk.

ICMR Guidelines
All nutritional targets are derived from authoritative Indian sources.

ICMR–NIN Recommended Dietary Allowances (2020)

The Indian Council of Medical Research and National Institute of Nutrition, Hyderabad, publishes Recommended Dietary Allowances specific to Indian physiology and dietary patterns. Poshan Path uses these RDAs to calculate age-appropriate portion targets, generate assessment scores, and flag nutritional deficiencies.

Tray Reference System

The Poshan Path standardised tray (400×280×40mm) is designed so that a fully filled tray meets the upper primary caloric target. Compartment volumes are calibrated to standard food densities published in the NIN Food Composition Database for India.

Disclaimer

Poshan Path is an educational and monitoring tool. All assessments are estimates based on visual and volumetric analysis. This application does not replace clinical dietary assessment by a qualified nutritionist or registered dietitian. For medical concerns related to child nutrition, consult a paediatrician.
